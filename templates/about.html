How does Trump tweets generator work?


Trump tweets generator uses AI/machine learning to generate new tweets from a sample (training data) of 36,000+ tweets. This process is called "Natural Language Generation". This is a difficult area of machine learning because most ML models are primarily used for prediction. However, we don't simply want to predict the order of words, because they will sound exactly like the sample. The key is to introduce some sense of randomness, without completely losing grammatical structure.


This algorithm uses bigram generation to understand the relationship bewteen words. Bigrams are simply the frequency ranking of words that occur after any word in a corpus. For example, the word you could have a bigram matrix of [["are", 90], ["were", 10]]. In this example, the word you is followed by the word are 90% of the time, and were 10% of the time. As you generate each word in a sequence, you then access the bigram matrix for that word, and select a new word that will come after it.


If you simply sample the most common word in each bigram matrix, the sequence generation will eventually loop the same phrase. This tool uses gamma distributions to sample from the bigram matrix so that the most common word is not always chosen. Different words and parts of speech trigger different distributions, so that text is generated with some coherence, yet also some randomness.
